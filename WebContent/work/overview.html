As an undergrad at the University of Pennsylvania I did not know what I wanted to do after school until I worked as a security intern at Accenture. After that summer I focused my remaining course work and research time into Computer/Information Security. It was a move that would serve me well in my career.

I was a consultant and professional software developer for about six years after I finished my undergraduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania. As my professional life flourished I developed a talent for solving business problems by devising creative solutions that leverage existing code modules and/or technology. This does not mean I only worked on modifying existing systems, far from it. I helped design and build a very large Single Sign On system for a large government entity, as well as many large independent add-ons to other web systems. I also developed/configured extensions for different large out-of-the-box (OOTB) systems for clients. I managed and produced deliverables in all phases of the standard software development life cycle, from design through implementation and support/training. I worked on highly visible client engagements and delivered assets that have resulted in reduced development time, cost, and risk while increasing quality and user satisfaction.
I have had the pleasure to work for Accenture, Deloitte & Touch LLC, and Tick Data, Inc.. While I was toiling away in the digital coal mines I specialized in: Object Oriented Development and Design, Identity and Access Management Design and Implementation, and Online Store Management.

I left the workforce to pursue my Master's Degree for two main reasons: I wanted to learn more about the fundamental aspects of Computer Science and I believed that I had reached a point in my work life where I would be able to best leverage what I learned. I am excited to use my skills acquired over the past 6 years in my graduate studies, and even more excited about the possibilities that lie ahead.
